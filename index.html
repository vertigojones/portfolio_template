I am a graduate of the Web Development Immersive at General Assembly, in Atlanta. Learning this skillset felt like a natural progression for me, coming from both a music and design background. Computers have always been a big part of what I do, and here’s how I got (t)here …

As a qualified music educator, I hit a point in my life where I wanted to combine ALL of my skills - music, design, and using a computer. So, I created a series of method books for the drum set (plus one "anti-method"), focusing on a one-on-one lesson environment between a teacher and student. The books are currently sold around the world on sites such as Amazon, enjoying rave reviews from customers.

Getting to know my computer and its applications very well, I reached the conclusion that I should spend more time (t)here. After the books were a success, I realized that I needed to create a website, using a host site with templates to create what I wanted. What started as a pet side-project turned into genuine interest, giving me something new and exciting to pour my creative talent into. And here I am, taking a new but confident step into the world of web development.

Looking forward, the world of web development is an exciting and inviting place to be. In addition to being fun and challenging, it’s something that I genuinely care about. After all, what better place to stage my talents than on the world wide web!

I am currently the in-house Web Developer at TripWire Interactive, a video game company in Roswell, GA, as well as the Front-End Product Developer at Azoozii, a local start-up in Atlanta that creates customizable memory books.
